Violinist Sean Mori

Described by the SF Classical Voice as “disarmingly talented”, violinist Sean Mori has performed extensively worldwide, giving concerts in Poland, Czech Republic, Switzerland, Japan, and all over the United States. A graduate of the San Francisco Conservatory of Music Pre-College under Professor Ian Swensen, he currently studies with Robin Sharp at Stanford.

Past accolades include being named a national YoungArts Prizewinner, performing on NPR’s radio show “From the Top”, being named a quarterfinalist at the 2018 Menuhin Competition, as well as winning first prize at the Pacific Musical Society competition, DVC/HNU Young Artist String Competition, North International Violin Competition,Nova Vista Concerto Competition, and Symphony Parnassus Concerto Competition. An avid chamber musician, he has won first prize and the Beethoven prize at the 2019 Pasadena Chamber Music Competition, first prize at the 2018 Galante competition, and was the first piano trio to win Gold at the National Fischoff Chamber Music Competition in 12 years. He has participated in many summer camps including Music@Menlo in 2015, Meadowmount School of Music in 2017, and received a full scholarship to the 22nd and 23rd Morningside Music Bridge Program hosted in Warsaw, Poland, and in Boston, Massachusetts.

Recent musical engagements include premiering Jake Heggie’s “Intonations” alongside violinist Daniel Hope and mezzo-soprano Sasha Cooke to critical acclaim, as well as recording for Pentatone records. He is a winner of the 2024 Stanford Concerto Competition, and will perform Bruch Violin Concerto alongside Stanford Philharmonia in May.
